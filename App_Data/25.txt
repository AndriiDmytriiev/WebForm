ğŸŒ¬ Atmosphere
Neptune's atmosphere is made up mainly of hydrogen and helium, but it also contains methane, which gives the planet its distinctive blue color. The methane absorbs red light and reflects blue light, making Neptune appear a striking shade of blue when viewed from space.

High Winds: Neptune is famous for its extreme winds, some of the fastest in the Solar System, reaching speeds of up to 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph). These winds help create Neptune's dynamic weather patterns.

Storms: One of Neptune's most famous atmospheric features is the Great Dark Spot, a massive storm system similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot. This storm was observed by Voyager 2 in 1989 but had disappeared by the time of later observations. The planet also experiences smaller storm systems that can be just as violent.

Clouds: Neptune's atmosphere contains high-altitude clouds made of methane ice, and lower-altitude clouds composed of ammonia ice. These clouds contribute to the planet's ever-changing appearance.
